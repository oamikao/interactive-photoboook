Web Design Research

May 20, 2026

An Article I chose to look at that relates to our course is Mobile-First CSS: Is It Time for a Rethink?, Written by Patrick Clancy. He argues the benefits of ditching open media queries and designing for mobile first, instead writing code without expecting to overwrite it. I found it interesting that people still discuss these things about html and style sheets years after they are developed, which shows how the community is still engaging with their tools and discovering new things. Since we learned about mobile designing first in the class, I think it is easier to override mobile designs through media queries, but Clancy makes a good point with avoiding prioritizing views for certain screens as we go more in-depth with desktop designs. Ultimately, simplicity is the best way for a site to be accessible, but css styles will definitely help with that when done right.

Tying the article and the Why CSS is so Weird video to what I understand, I truly do find web design to be somewhat inspiring due to its “accessibility first” nature. Although it's more for computers to read and convey information rather than accessibility for humans, it highlights the importance of content over styles, yet still both feel equally important. UI UX design is extremely structured yet flexible enough where millions of websites can be so different, and that is definitely my favorite part; being able to take basic tools and create something interesting and functional.
